31 STEPS

1 If your customer doesn't want or does not have AWS Business Level Support, create a Pass Through Support Plan Group in SES

2 The Pass Through Support Plan Group will pass the Support cost at Zero margin to the Reseller. This means, if the customer decides to activate any Support services, the support cost will pass to the customer, via the support plan group you set up

3 Please Note: If the customer activates Support services, and you do not have the Pass Through Support Plan enabled, will result in negative margins in the AWS Billing Reports

4 The negative margins in the AWS Billing Reports is because you are absorbing the cost of Support, instead of passing the support cost to your customer, hence the Support Plan Group setup is necessary 

5 The first step is to open StreamOne Enterprise Solutions and click the Customers module

6 Select your customer in the list, by checking the Box next to the Customer Name.  Alternatively, you can just double-click on the Customer's name

7 For my example, I have checked the box next to the customer's name. Click the EDIT button to continue

8 Expand the Customer Billing folder on the left side, by clicking the drop-down arrow

9 Click Charges and discounts

10 Click Amazon Web Services

11 When you select Amazon Web Services, the Assign button appears. Click ASSIGN

12 Click CREATE GROUP

13 Enter a Support Plan Group name.  For my example, I will Type AWS Pass through, No Support - Customer. Click the rectangle under the 'Name' field to continue

14 Click ADD

15 Click the box next to Charges and discounts pass-through

16 Click Ok

17 Click SAVE

18 To view the AWS accounts that are currently not assigned to a Support Plan Group, Click the Arrow button next to Unassigned

19 Check the Box next to Unassigned, to select all accounts or select only specific accounts

20 To move the accounts to the new Support Plan Group, Click MOVE TO

21 Click on the Support Plan Group Name in the list

22 To confirm the accounts have moved to the new group, Click the Arrow button next to the Support Plan Group Name, to expand the list

23 Click SAVE.  
That's it!  You are done.

Now let's take a look at the SES Knowledge Base

24 If you would like to read the SES Knowledge Base Articles, Click the Question mark, in the upper right hand corner

25 click Support or  Documentation. For this example, let's click the Support button

26 This is the SES Support Ticket creation window. If support is needed, open a support ticket and provide the details of support needed.
Let's continue to the SES Knowledge Base, by Clicking Search Articles

27 Click or Browse articles

28 To look for a specific topic, Enter your search term under the 'How can we help you today' header.  

29 Another way to find a How To article, is to scroll through  the SES Knowledge Base and search by the SES Module. Click the highlighted rectangle to continue

30 Click on a Article Name that appears in the list

31 Here's your first article to read. Feel free to browse the SES Knowledge Base for more How To articles.  
That's it. You're done.
